Who This Is For

This offering is designed for women who:

  • Feel off but can't quite explain why
  • Have labs that never fully explained how they feel
  • Are tired of trying random supplements or advice
  • Want professional guidance without extremes
  • Are preparing for a new season (preconception, pregnancy, postpartum, or personal reset)
  • Value a calm, structured approach to health
"If you want a plan - but you want it built after understanding the full picture - this was created for you."

  • how you're feeling
  • how you're living

What's Included

1

Comprehensive Foundations Intake

A detailed health intake that looks at symptoms, lifestyle patterns, stress, sleep, nutrition, supplements, and goals - creating the clinical context for everything that follows.

2

Foundational Laboratory Evaluation

You don't need to decide what to test. If you've had recent labs, they'll be reviewed. If you haven't, a comprehensive foundational lab panel will be ordered. Additional labs may be added as clinically appropriate.

"Labs are used to support assessment - not to chase numbers."

3

Hair Tissue Mineral Analysis (HTMA)

HTMA is used as an adjunctive tool to observe mineral patterns and stress physiology over time, interpreted alongside symptoms, lifestyle, and blood labs.

4

60-Minute Interpretation & Planning Visit

During this visit, we review labs and HTMA in plain language, connect findings to how you actually feel, identify your top priorities, clarify what matters now vs. later, and build a clear, personalized protocol.

5

Personalized Foundations Protocol

You'll receive a written care plan that may include nutrition guidance, lifestyle and daily rhythm support, stress and nervous system considerations, supplement education when appropriate, and a clear implementation order.

The goal is clarity - not overload.

6

Integration Period (6-8 Weeks)

You'll have time to implement changes intentionally and allow your body to respond before reassessment.

7

Follow-Up HTMA & Reassessment Visit

A second HTMA provides a directional snapshot, paired with a follow-up visit to review progress, reflect on symptom changes, adjust priorities if needed, and discuss optional next steps.
