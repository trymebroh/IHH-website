Meet Alicia!

Hi, I'm Alicia—a wife, mama, and board-certified Family Nurse Practitioner.

If you've found your way here, you're probably a woman who's tired of feeling like something is "off" but can't quite figure out what it is. Maybe you're struggling with low energy, irregular cycles, stubborn skin issues, or mood swings that seem to come out of nowhere.

Maybe you've been told your labs are "normal," but deep down, you know your body is trying to tell you something.

I see you. I hear you. And I get it—because I've been there too.

Becoming a mama in 2024 made me even more passionate about supporting women through preconception, pregnancy, and postpartum—not just surviving these stages, but actually thriving through them.

My goal is to help you feel educated, and empowered so healthy habits actually stick—without the overwhelm.

My Pappy - where it all began

A Passion For Holistic Healing

My passion for whole-body wellness started long before nursing school. Growing up, I watched my Pappy navigate Parkinson's disease and type 2 diabetes—and I saw firsthand how diet and lifestyle changes made a real difference in his quality of life.

That experience planted a seed: What if we could prevent disease before it starts?

I earned my Bachelor's in Nursing and spent years in critical care, but something kept pulling me toward a bigger picture approach. Then, my own health started sending signals I couldn't ignore—fatigue, breakouts, cycles that didn't make sense.

Traditional medicine offered band-aid solutions, but I wanted answers. So, I dove into functional medicine, root-cause analysis, and holistic nutrition—and everything changed.

Back to the Basics

I discovered that hormones don't exist in a vacuum—they're connected to metabolism, stress, nutrition, and daily habits. When we address the foundations, the body has an incredible ability to heal.

That realization led me to earn my Master of Science in Nursing and become a Family Nurse Practitioner. But I didn't stop there.

I pursued advanced education in nutrition and learned to use laboratory interpretation tools like Hair Tissue Mineral Analysis (HTMA) to uncover hidden imbalances—especially for women navigating conception, pregnancy, and postpartum.
